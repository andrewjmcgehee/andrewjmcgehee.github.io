About

Research Interests

My research interests lie broadly in the fields of artificial general intelligence and machine learning. The application domain is less important to me than the exploration of novel, state of the art A.I. applications and theory. That said, I am particularly interested in deep reinforcement learning and the role of the default mode network in the human brain. It is my firm belief that the most promising way forward in artificial intelligence research is through a deep understanding of the human brain and nervous system as both a densely intricate array of sensors and a highly organized filtration system for those sensory inputs.

My current work is diverse, spanning the subfields of bioinformatics, deep learning, reinforcement learning, evolutionary computing, and neuroscience. My ultimate goal is to conduct interdisciplinary research in computational neuroscience and deep reinforcement learning. Since I do not update this website often, the most current and accurate representation of my work is my curriculum vitae.
